Welcome to the Milky Way Galaxy

Discover the Milky Way

The Galaxy (Milky Way): A massive bar-spiral galaxy approximately 100,000 to 150,000 light-years in diameter, comprising gas, dust, and stars rotating around a supermassive black hole, Sagittarius A*, at its center.
Our Location (Orion Arm): We are located in the suburbs of the galaxy, about 25,000–26,000 light-years from the center, situated on a smaller spiral arm called the Orion Arm (or Orion Spur).
The Neighborhood (Local Interstellar Cloud): Within the Orion Arm, our solar system travels through a low-density region of the interstellar medium known as the Local Interstellar Cloud.
Our Home (The Sol System): Our system consists of the Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, and countless smaller bodies (asteroids and comets), with the furthest known objects reaching into the Oort Cloud, almost a light-year away.
