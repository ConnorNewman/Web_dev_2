Sol system

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the masses that orbit it, most prominently its eight planets, of which Earth is one. The system formed about 4.6 billion years ago when a dense region of a molecular cloud collapsed, creating the Sun and a protoplanetary disc from which the orbiting bodies assembled.

The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the Solar System's total mass. Inside the Sun's core, hydrogen is fused into helium, releasing energy that is emitted through the Sun's photosphere.

Closest to the Sun are Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars. Earth and Mars lie within the habitable zone.

Beyond this are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Jupiter and Saturn contain most of the remaining mass.

The planets of our solar system

Mercury: Smallest planet, closest to the Sun.

Venus: Hottest planet with dense atmosphere.

Earth: Supports life with liquid water.

Mars: The red planet.

Jupiter: Largest planet.

Saturn: Known for its rings.

Uranus: Tilted ice giant.

Neptune: Cold and windy.

Pluto: Since 2006 classified as a dwarf planet.
