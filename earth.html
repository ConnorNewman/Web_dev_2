Earth - Our Home

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. It formed approximately 4.54 billion years ago and is the densest, largest, and most massive of the four rocky terrestrial planets.

Key Details About Earth:

  • Structure: Layered interior with inner core, outer core, mantle, and crust.
  • Orbit & Rotation: Orbits the Sun in 365.25 days and rotates every 23 hours 56 minutes.
  • Atmosphere: Mostly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%).
  • Life: Life emerged at least 3.5 billion years ago.
  • Moon: Has one natural satellite – the Moon.

For more information: Wikipedia - Earth

How Earth and the Moon Formed

Earth formed from gas and dust orbiting the young Sun. Shortly after, a Mars-sized protoplanet called Theia collided with Earth in a giant impact.

Key Details of Formation:

  • The Giant Impact: Theia struck Earth, melting both bodies.
  • Moon Formation: Debris from the collision formed a ring that coalesced into the Moon.
  • Scientific Consensus: The Giant Impact Hypothesis is the leading theory.
